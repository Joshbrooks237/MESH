Understanding Hemorrhoids

What Are Hemorrhoids?

Hemorrhoids are swollen veins in the lower rectum and anus, similar to varicose veins. They can develop internally (inside the rectum) or externally (under the skin around the anus).

Common Symptoms

  • Bright red blood on toilet paper or in stool
  • Itching or irritation in the anal area
  • Pain or discomfort during bowel movements
  • Swelling around the anus
  • Feeling of incomplete bowel emptying

Risk Factors

  • Straining during bowel movements
  • Chronic constipation or diarrhea
  • Sitting for long periods
  • Low-fiber diet
  • Pregnancy and childbirth
  • Obesity

Understanding Constipation

What Is Constipation?

Constipation occurs when bowel movements become less frequent and stools become difficult to pass. It happens when the colon absorbs too much water from waste, making it hard and dry.

Common Symptoms

  • Fewer than three bowel movements per week
  • Hard, dry, or lumpy stools
  • Straining during bowel movements
  • Feeling of incomplete evacuation
  • Abdominal pain or bloating

Causes

  • Low-fiber diet
  • Inadequate water intake
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Medications (painkillers, antidepressants)
  • Ignoring the urge to have a bowel movement
  • Changes in routine or diet

Understanding Diarrhea

What Is Diarrhea?

Diarrhea is characterized by loose, watery stools occurring more frequently than normal (typically 3+ times daily). It can range from mild and self-limiting to severe and life-threatening.

Common Symptoms

  • Loose, watery stools
  • Urgent need to have bowel movements
  • Abdominal cramps and pain
  • Bloating and gas
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Dehydration symptoms (dry mouth, dizziness)

Types of Diarrhea

  • Acute: Sudden onset, usually from infection (viral, bacterial, parasitic)
  • Chronic: Lasts 4+ weeks, may indicate underlying condition
  • Traveler's: From contaminated food/water in new environments
  • Antibiotic-associated: From antibiotic use disrupting gut flora
  • Functional: Related to IBS, stress, or food intolerances

Common Causes

  • Infections: Viral (norovirus, rotavirus), bacterial (E. coli, Salmonella)
  • Food sensitivities: Lactose intolerance, gluten sensitivity
  • Medications: Antibiotics, chemotherapy drugs, laxatives
  • Digestive disorders: IBS, IBD, celiac disease
  • Dietary factors: Excessive alcohol, caffeine, artificial sweeteners
  • Stress and anxiety: Can trigger gut-brain axis response

Dehydration Risks

  • Loss of electrolytes (sodium, potassium)
  • Reduced blood volume
  • Impaired kidney function
  • Muscle cramps and weakness
  • Low blood pressure
  • Electrolyte imbalances

When to Seek Help

  • Blood in stool or vomit
  • Signs of severe dehydration
  • High fever (over 101.5¬∞F)
  • Diarrhea lasting more than 3 days
  • Symptoms in infants or elderly
  • Recent travel to high-risk areas
